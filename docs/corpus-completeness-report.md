## 7. ACQUISITION STRATEGIES

### Strategy A: Public Domain Text Harvesting (LOW COST, HIGH IMPACT)

**Target:** All texts published before ~1928 (US copyright) or by authors who died before ~1955 (life+70 in most jurisdictions).

**Sources:**
1. **HebrewBooks.org** - Download PDFs of PD seforim, run OCR, structure into your format
2. **Internet Archive** - Similar approach; many Hebrew books scanned
3. **HathiTrust** - PD materials freely accessible
4. **Wikisource Hebrew** - Some community-transcribed texts
5. **Project Ben Yehuda** - Hebrew literary texts (PD)

**Covers:** Most Rishonim, Acharonim through ~1900, all Geonic literature, classical responsa, SA commentaries, Rambam commentaries, Targumim, ancient texts

**Technical pipeline:** PDF -> OCR (Tesseract/custom Hebrew model) -> Text correction (crowdsourced or AI-assisted) -> Structural markup -> Integration

### Strategy B: Open-Access Partnership (MEDIUM COST, HIGH IMPACT)

**Potential partners:**
1. **Friedberg Jewish Manuscript Society** - Already free; negotiate data access
2. **Sefaria** - Already open source; ensure complete fork is current
3. **Open Siddur Project** - TEI XML data, open license
4. **National Library of Israel (NLI)** - Ktiv images, potential transcription partnerships
5. **Cambridge Digital Library** - Genizah images and metadata
6. **University of Pennsylvania (OPenn)** - Genizah and medieval manuscripts
7. **Leon Levy Dead Sea Scrolls Digital Library** - Image access (transcription rights may vary)

### Strategy C: Community Crowdsourcing (LOW COST, MEDIUM-LONG TERM)

**Model:** Wikipedia/Sefaria-style volunteer transcription
- Build a transcription tool
- Recruit from yeshiva/university communities
- Focus on high-value missing texts
- Quality assurance through double-entry and expert review

### Strategy D: AI-Assisted Digitization (MEDIUM COST, TRANSFORMATIVE)

**Technologies:**
1. **Hebrew/Aramaic OCR** - Train models on HebrewBooks corpus
2. **Handwritten Text Recognition (HTR)** - For manuscript transcription
3. **Automatic cross-reference detection** - NLP models to find citations
4. **Named Entity Recognition** - Identify rabbis, places, texts mentioned
5. **Translation assistance** - AI draft translations with human review

### Strategy E: Licensed Content (HIGH COST, COPYRIGHT)

For modern texts still under copyright:
1. Negotiate directly with publishers/estates
2. Seek philanthropic funding for licensing
3. Some publishers (Artscroll, Koren) may license for non-commercial use
4. Israeli law may differ from US copyright on some older texts
5. Consider "fair use" scholarly annotation (citations/references without full text)
